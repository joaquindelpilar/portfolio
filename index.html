Project RadioVax: Optimizing the Synergy Between Targeted Radiation and mRNA Immunotherapy

A featured project

Designed a conceptual framework evaluating the combined efficacy of localized radiation therapy and systemic mRNA personalized vaccines. The project explores the biological mechanism where radiation-induced tumor cell death releases neoantigens, effectively acting as an "in-situ" vaccine. By mapping out a proposed treatment timeline, this project models how to time mRNA vaccine delivery to capitalize on the immune system's heightened response post-radiation. The goal of the framework is to theorize a protocol that maximizes primary tumor destruction while minimizing the risk of systemic metastasis and relapse.

Project Solis Pavilion: Climate-Responsive Urban Architecture

Another selected work

Conceptualized and drafted a mixed-use community hub designed to mitigate the urban heat island effect. Utilizing biomimetic principles, the building's facade features adaptive, kinetic shading that adjusts to the sun's trajectory throughout the day to optimize passive cooling and natural daylight.
